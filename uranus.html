Solar System Explorer
URANUS

Uranus is an ice giant with a bluish-green color due to methane gas in its atmosphere. Unlike other planets, it rotates on its side, possibly due to a massive collision in the past. This gives Uranus extreme seasons, with 42 years of daylight and 42 years of darkness at its poles.
It has thin, dark rings, not as visible as Saturn’s but still present. Uranus’s atmosphere contains icy clouds, and its winds can reach 900 km/h (560 mph). Scientists believe it has an icy mantle surrounding a rocky core.

  • Uranus has 27 moons, mostly named after Shakespearean characters.
  • It is the coldest planet, with temperatures dropping to -224°C (-371°F).
  • It takes 84 Earth years to orbit the Sun.
