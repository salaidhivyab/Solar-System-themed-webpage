JUPITER

Jupiter is the largest planet, made mostly of gas. It has a massive storm system, the Great Red Spot, which has been raging for centuries. Its strong gravity pulls in space debris.
With over 79 moons, Jupiter’s largest moon, Ganymede, is even bigger than Mercury. Some moons, like Europa, may have subsurface oceans and could host alien life.

  • Jupiter’s atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium.
  • It has the strongest magnetic field of all planets.
  • Its gravity influences many asteroids and comets.
